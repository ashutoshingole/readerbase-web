Privacy Policy

Effective date: May 3, 2026

At Readerbase, we believe your reading journey and personal reflections belong to you. This Privacy Policy explains how the Readerbase application ("App") handles information. The app is designed as a private, offline-first library on your device. We do not operate our own servers for your books or notes, and we do not sell personal information.

1. Data collection and local storage

We do not collect or store your library or notes on our servers. Readerbase does not require an account, login, or password. Because we do not run a backend that holds your content, we cannot access:

  • The books you add to your library
  • The custom tags or shelves you create
  • The articles you save or read in your Feed
  • The private notes or excerpts you keep in your Vault

All of this information stays on your device's internal storage (iOS or Android) unless you explicitly export it.

2. Device permissions

The App requests permissions only for the features below. Processing stays on your device:

  • Camera: used only to scan ISBN barcodes on physical books. We do not record video, store photos for our use, or send image data to our servers.
  • Biometrics (Face ID, Touch ID, fingerprint): if you lock the app or Vault with biometrics, your operating system performs authentication. The App receives only a success or failure result and cannot read your biometric data.
  • Storage or files: used when you choose to export a backup of your library or restore from a backup file.

3. Network requests and third-party services

Your library and Vault content stay on your device, but the App uses the internet for some tasks:

  • Book metadata and covers: when you search by title, author, or ISBN, the App queries public book databases (such as Google Books or Open Library) for details like title, author, description, and cover art.
  • Article feeds: when you refresh your Feed, the App downloads content from the RSS feeds and sources you configured.
  • Reading articles: when you open an article, content may load in an in-app browser or viewer. Third-party publishers may use cookies or similar technologies under their own policies.

When the App contacts external services, those providers may log standard technical data (for example, your IP address), similar to visiting their website in any browser. Readerbase does not operate those servers and does not keep a log of your searches on our side.

4. Backups and data portability

Because data is stored locally, uninstalling the App or losing your device can delete your content unless you have a backup. You may export your data from the App and save files on your device or upload them to a cloud provider you trust (for example, iCloud or Google Drive). Once your files are with another service, that provider's terms and privacy policy govern them.

5. Analytics and advertising

Readerbase is free and community-focused. We do not monetize personal data.

  • No analytics SDKs: we do not use third-party analytics tools to track your behavior, screen views, or time spent in the App.
  • No ads: we do not show advertisements or integrate third-party ad networks.

6. Children's privacy

The App is not directed at children to collect data, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone, including children under 13. Since content is not uploaded to our servers, information remains on the device under the control of the device owner.

7. Your privacy rights and regional information

Depending on where you live, privacy laws may grant you rights over personal data. Readerbase processes your library and notes locally on your device and does not maintain user accounts or a central copy of your content on our servers. You can access, export, or delete your data using the App and your device's storage settings. If you contact us through Send Feedback, we only receive what you choose to include in your message. California residents: we do not sell personal information as defined under the CCPA/CPRA.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this Privacy Policy when features change how data is handled (for example, if optional cloud sync is introduced in the future). We will update the effective date and show the revised policy in the App. Please review this page from time to time.

9. Contact us

Questions or concerns? Use Send Feedback in the app menu. Your email app will open so you can reach us directly. We do not operate a backend inbox inside the App.
